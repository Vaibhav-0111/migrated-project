1. PLATFORM OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Calm Quest is an interactive 3D math learning platform designed specifically
for students with ADHD and diverse learning needs. It transforms abstract
mathematical concepts into hands-on, visual, and tactile 3D experiences.

Key Philosophy: "Intuition before math"
→ Students FEEL the math through physical interaction BEFORE encountering
  formulas or symbolic notation.

Each chapter follows a strict 3-game structure:
  • Game 1 — Easy (Introductory concept)
  • Game 2 — Medium (Deeper application)
  • Game 3 — Advanced (Real-world or complex scenario)

Every game follows this flow:
  Tutorial → 3D Animation → Interactive Play → Step-by-Step Help → Quiz

================================================================================
2. HOW THE LEARNING SYSTEM WORKS
================================================================================

Step 1: TUTORIAL
  A friendly explanation introduces the topic in plain English.
  No formulas yet — just the core idea.

Step 2: 3D ANIMATION
  A short animated scene demonstrates the concept visually.
  Example: A shadow growing/shrinking as the sun moves.

Step 3: INTERACTIVE PLAY
  The student takes control. They drag sliders, click objects, adjust
  parameters, and see real-time visual feedback in a 3D scene.

Step 4: STEP-BY-STEP HELPER
  If stuck, a guided walkthrough breaks the problem into small steps.
  Each step has a hint and visual cue.

Step 5: AI-GENERATED QUIZ (8 Questions)
  After completing the game, an 8-question quiz tests comprehension.
  Questions are AI-generated, grade-specific (Grade 11 or 12), and
  written in plain, human-readable English.
  Fallback: Curated static questions if AI is unavailable.

3. CHAPTER 1 — TRIGONOMETRY
================================================================================

The Trigonometry chapter teaches angles, sine/cosine relationships, and the
Pythagorean identity through real-world scenarios.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 1: Shadow Garden Challenge (Easy)
Route: /game/angle-shadow-garden
Topic: Angles & Shadow Length
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY SHADOWS?
  Shadows are the most natural, everyday example of trigonometry. Every
  student has noticed that shadows change length during the day. This game
  connects that everyday observation to the mathematical relationship:
  shadow length = object height × tan(angle).

WHAT HAPPENS:
  A 3D garden scene contains a tree and a bench. The sun floats in the sky.
  The student adjusts the sun's angle using a slider. As the angle changes,
  the tree's shadow stretches or shrinks in real-time.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Read the tutorial explaining how sun angle affects shadow length
  2. Watch the animation showing the shadow changing
  3. Use the slider to adjust the sun angle
  4. Goal: Position the sun so the shadow perfectly covers the bench
  5. Target angle: ~60 degrees (tolerance: ±3°)
  6. After success, complete the 8-question quiz

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • The relationship between angle and shadow length
  • tan(angle) = opposite/adjacent (shadow length / tree height)
  • Why shadows are short at noon (high angle) and long at sunset (low angle)
  • Practical trigonometry without memorizing formulas first

MATHEMATICAL CONNECTION:
  tan(θ) = opposite / adjacent
  Shadow length = Height / tan(θ)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 2: Mountain Rope Rescue (Medium)
Route: /game/mountain-rope-rescue
Topic: Sine Function & Right Triangles
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY A RESCUE MISSION?
  The sine function describes the relationship between an angle and the
  ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse. A rescue scenario makes
  this vivid: the drone angle determines how much rope you need to reach
  the stranded climber. Too steep = not enough rope. Too shallow = rope
  doesn't reach. There's a perfect angle — and that's sine in action.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  A 3D mountain scene with a cliff, a stranded climber, and a rescue drone.
  The drone hovers at a variable angle. A rope extends from the drone
  toward the climber. The student adjusts the drone angle to find the
  exact position where the rope reaches the climber.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial explains sine = opposite / hypotenuse
  2. Watch the animated rescue attempt
  3. Drag the slider to adjust the drone's angle
  4. The rope length changes based on sin(angle)
  5. Target angle: ~55 degrees (tolerance: ±4°)
  6. Complete the 8-question quiz on sine calculations

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • sin(θ) = opposite / hypotenuse
  • How changing an angle affects the "reach" of the hypotenuse
  • Real-world applications of sine (engineering, rescue, navigation)

MATHEMATICAL CONNECTION:
  sin(θ) = Height / Rope Length
  Rope Length = Height / sin(θ)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 3: Wave Balance Lab (Advanced)
Route: /game/wave-balance-lab
Topic: Pythagorean Identity (sin²θ + cos²θ = 1)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY WAVES?
  The Pythagorean identity is one of the most elegant truths in
  trigonometry, but it's abstract. By visualizing sin²θ and cos²θ as
  two energy waves that always sum to 100%, students physically
  experience conservation — as one wave grows, the other shrinks,
  but together they're always complete.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  Two glowing energy bars represent sin²θ and cos²θ. As the student
  adjusts the angle θ, one bar grows while the other shrinks. A total
  energy meter always reads 100% (or 1.0). The challenge: find the
  angle where both bars are exactly equal (45°).

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial explains sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  2. Watch the animated energy transfer between the two waves
  3. Adjust θ with the slider
  4. Watch sin² and cos² values update in real-time
  5. Goal: Find the angle where sin²θ = cos²θ = 0.5 (answer: 45°)
  6. Complete the quiz on the Pythagorean identity

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 (always, for any angle)
  • At 45°, sin and cos are equal
  • Energy conservation as a metaphor for mathematical identity
  • The unit circle relationship

MATHEMATICAL CONNECTION:
  sin²(45°) = 0.5
  cos²(45°) = 0.5
  sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 (for all θ)

4. CHAPTER 2 — ALGEBRA
================================================================================

The Algebra chapter teaches equation solving, algebraic manipulation, and
pattern recognition through physical, tactile 3D interactions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 1: Balance Garden (Easy)
Route: /game/balance-garden
Topic: Solving Linear Equations (Balancing)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY A GARDEN BALANCE?
  An equation is fundamentally about balance — the left side equals the
  right side. A physical balance/scale is the perfect metaphor. Students
  add and remove "weights" from both sides, experiencing that whatever
  you do to one side, you MUST do to the other. This builds algebraic
  intuition before symbolic manipulation.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  A 3D balance scale with blocks on both sides. One side has a glowing
  "mystery box" (the variable x) plus some number blocks. The other side
  has only number blocks. The student removes blocks from both sides
  equally until only the mystery box remains — revealing its value.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "An equation is like a balance scale"
  2. See the equation displayed: e.g., x + 2 = 5
  3. Progressive rounds with increasing difficulty:
     Round 1: x + 2 = 5 (remove 2 from both sides → x = 3)
     Round 2: 2x = 6 (split both sides by 2 → x = 3)
     Round 3: x + 4 = 9 (remove 4 → x = 5)
     Round 4: 3x + 1 = 10 (remove 1, then divide by 3 → x = 3)
  4. Click "Remove from both sides" or "Divide both sides"
  5. Complete quiz after all 4 rounds

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • Equations mean "both sides are equal"
  • To solve: isolate the variable by doing the same thing to both sides
  • Addition/subtraction and multiplication/division as inverse operations

MATHEMATICAL CONNECTION:
  x + a = b → x = b - a
  ax = b → x = b / a

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 2: Equation Factory (Medium)
Route: /game/equation-factory
Topic: Multi-Step Equation Solving
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY A FACTORY?
  Factories process things step by step on conveyor belts. This is exactly
  how multi-step equations work — you perform operations one at a time,
  in the right order, to isolate the variable. The conveyor belt metaphor
  makes the sequential nature of algebra visible and physical.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  A 3D factory with two conveyor belts separated by an equals sign. The
  left belt carries a glowing purple variable cube (x) and blue constant
  blocks. The right belt carries blue constant blocks. Two action buttons
  let students manipulate both sides simultaneously:
  • "➖ Remove 1 from both" — subtracts one constant block from each side
  • "÷ Divide by [coefficient]" — divides the variable coefficient
  A live equation display updates in real-time as operations are applied,
  with dynamic step hints guiding the student through the process.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Whatever you do to one belt, do to the other"
  2. Round 1: x + 3 = 7 → Tap "Remove" 3 times → x = 4
  3. Round 2: 2x + 4 = 10 → Remove 4, then divide by 2 → x = 3
  4. Round 3: 3x + 2 = 11 → Remove 2, then divide by 3 → x = 3
  5. Round 4: 4x + 5 = 17 → Remove 5, then divide by 4 → x = 3
  6. Dynamic step hints: "Remove constants first" → "Now divide"
  7. Tap "Check Solution" to verify — correct answers advance rounds
  8. Step-by-Step Helper available via the help button (shows worked
     example with current equation state)
  9. Complete the 8-question quiz after all 4 rounds

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • Order of operations in reverse (undo addition first, then division)
  • Multi-step equation solving with progressive difficulty
  • The golden rule: "Do the same thing to both sides"
  • Strategic thinking: constants first, then coefficients
  • Visual confirmation that the equation stays balanced throughout

MATHEMATICAL CONNECTION:
  ax + b = c
  Step 1: ax = c - b (remove constant from both sides)
  Step 2: x = (c - b) / a (divide both sides by coefficient)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 3: Pattern Portal World (Advanced)
Route: /game/pattern-portal
Topic: Patterns, Sequences & Substitution
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY PORTALS?
  Patterns and sequences are about transformation — something goes in,
  something comes out, following a hidden rule. Portals are the perfect
  visual metaphor: objects enter a portal and emerge changed. The student
  must discover the transformation rule and predict the next output.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  Floating portals in a mystical 3D space. Objects (colored spheres with
  numbers) enter portals and emerge transformed. The student observes
  the pattern (e.g., each number doubles: 1, 2, 4, 8) and selects the
  correct sequence from available options.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Portals follow hidden rules — discover them!"
  2. Watch objects transform through the portal
  3. Available numbers appear as clickable orbs
  4. Select the correct sequence: 1, 2, 4, 8
  5. Each correct selection lights up a portal
  6. Complete the quiz on pattern recognition

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • Recognizing mathematical patterns (geometric, arithmetic)
  • Substitution: if f(x) = 2x, then f(1) = 2, f(2) = 4, etc.
  • Predicting terms in a sequence
  • The foundation of algebraic functions

MATHEMATICAL CONNECTION:
  Geometric sequence: aₙ = a₁ × rⁿ⁻¹
  Example: 1, 2, 4, 8 → r = 2

5. CHAPTER 3 — VOLUME & SURFACE AREA
================================================================================

This chapter teaches 3D measurement through hands-on building, filling,
and wrapping of virtual objects.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 1: Shape Fill Playground (Easy)
Route: /game/shape-fill-playground
Topic: Volume of Basic 3D Shapes
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY FILLING SHAPES?
  Volume is "how much stuff fits inside." Rather than memorizing formulas,
  students literally fill 3D shapes with virtual liquid and see how much
  fits. The visceral experience of watching liquid rise inside a cube vs.
  a cylinder vs. a sphere builds intuition about relative volumes.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  3D shapes (cube, cylinder, sphere) appear one at a time. A virtual tap
  pours liquid into each shape. The student adjusts the fill level using
  a slider. A meter shows the current fill percentage.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Volume = how much fits inside"
  2. Shape 1 (Cube): Fill to 70% — see how volume relates to side length
  3. Shape 2 (Cylinder): Fill to 70% — compare πr²h to cube volume
  4. Shape 3 (Sphere): Fill to 70% — see (4/3)πr³ in action
  5. Complete all 3 shapes, then take the quiz

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • Volume is a 3D measurement
  • Different shapes with similar dimensions hold different amounts
  • Cube: V = s³, Cylinder: V = πr²h, Sphere: V = (4/3)πr³

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 2: Wrap the Gift Studio (Medium)
Route: /game/wrap-gift-studio
Topic: Surface Area of 3D Shapes
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY GIFT WRAPPING?
  Surface area is "how much wrapping paper you need." This is the most
  relatable real-world analogy. Students click on each face of a 3D
  shape to "wrap" it, experiencing that surface area = sum of all faces.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  3D gift boxes (cube, rectangular prism, triangular prism) appear.
  Each face is a clickable panel. The student clicks faces one by one
  to cover them with wrapping paper. A progress meter tracks coverage.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Surface area = total area of all faces"
  2. Shape 1 (Cube): Click all 6 faces (each s² → total 6s²)
  3. Shape 2 (Rectangular): Click all 6 faces (2lw + 2lh + 2wh)
  4. Shape 3 (Triangular prism): Click 5 faces
  5. Complete all shapes, then take the quiz

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • Surface area = total area of all outer faces
  • Cubes have 6 identical square faces
  • Rectangular prisms have 3 pairs of identical rectangular faces
  • The relationship between surface area and volume

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 3: Build & Balance City (Advanced)
Route: /game/build-balance-city
Topic: Volume vs. Surface Area Optimization
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY CITY BUILDING?
  Real architects and engineers must optimize: maximum interior space
  (volume) with minimum materials (surface area). This game turns that
  real-world challenge into a creative building experience.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  The student designs buildings by adjusting width, height, and depth
  sliders. Volume and surface area meters update in real-time. Three
  progressive goals challenge different optimization strategies.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Volume = storage space, Surface area = building materials"
  2. Goal 1 (Warehouse): Maximize volume ≥ 64 cubic units
  3. Goal 2 (Efficient): Good volume with minimum surface area
  4. Goal 3 (Balanced): Find the optimal ratio of volume to surface area
  5. Adjust width, height, depth sliders for each goal
  6. Complete the quiz

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • A cube has the best volume-to-surface-area ratio among rectangular prisms
  • Tall/thin shapes have high surface area relative to volume
  • Optimization: balancing competing mathematical constraints
  • Real-world engineering and architecture applications

6. CHAPTER 4 — PROBABILITY
================================================================================

The Probability chapter teaches chance, experimental vs. theoretical
probability, and prediction through interactive experiments.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 1: Chance Garden (Easy)
Route: /game/chance-garden
Topic: Basic Probability (Equally Likely Outcomes)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY A GARDEN?
  A garden with colored flowers is a natural, calming way to introduce
  probability. "If you randomly pick a flower, what color will it be?"
  The visual ratio of flower colors makes probability intuitive — more
  red flowers = higher chance of picking red.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  A 3D garden with colored flowers (baskets representing different
  outcomes). The student picks a basket based on a probability prompt.
  For example: "Which color has the best chance?" The correct answer
  is the basket with the most flowers of that color.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Probability = favorable outcomes / total outcomes"
  2. 3 rounds of increasing difficulty
  3. Each round: see colored baskets, read the question, pick the answer
  4. Correct picks earn points; incorrect picks give encouragement
  5. Complete 3 rounds, then take the quiz

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • Probability ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain)
  • P(event) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes
  • More of something = higher probability of selecting it

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 2: Spinner & Dice Lab (Medium)
Route: /game/spinner-dice-lab
Topic: Experimental Probability
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY SPINNERS AND DICE?
  Spinners and dice are the classic probability tools. This game bridges
  the gap between theoretical probability (what SHOULD happen) and
  experimental probability (what ACTUALLY happens when you try).

WHAT HAPPENS:
  A 3D spinner wheel with colored segments and a die. The student spins
  the wheel multiple times and records outcomes. A frequency table and
  bar chart update in real-time. The student then predicts the next
  outcome based on observed frequencies.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Spin many times and see what happens!"
  2. Click "Spin" to spin the wheel — watch it rotate and land
  3. Results are tracked in a live frequency chart
  4. After 10+ spins, predict which color will come up next
  5. Compare experimental frequencies to theoretical probabilities
  6. Complete the quiz

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • Experimental probability approaches theoretical probability
    with more trials (Law of Large Numbers)
  • How to record and interpret frequency data
  • That individual outcomes are unpredictable, but patterns emerge

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 3: Prediction Park (Advanced)
Route: /game/prediction-park
Topic: Experimental Probability & Data Analysis
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY A PREDICTION PARK?
  This game takes experimental probability further — students run
  multiple dice-rolling experiments, collect frequency data as 3D bar
  charts, and then predict outcomes. It connects experimental data
  to theoretical probability through hands-on experimentation.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  A 3D park with a giant rolling die and result pillars that grow
  based on frequency. The student rolls the die 10 times per round,
  watches the frequency bars grow, then predicts which number was
  rolled most frequently.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Roll, observe, predict!"
  2. Round 1: Roll 10 times → frequency bars grow → predict most common
  3. Round 2: New experiment with fresh data
  4. Round 3: Final prediction challenge
  5. Compare predictions to actual data
  6. Complete the quiz

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • How to conduct probability experiments
  • Reading and interpreting frequency distributions
  • Making predictions from experimental data
  • Why larger sample sizes give more reliable predictions

7. CHAPTER 5 — FRACTIONS, DECIMALS, PERCENTAGES & INTEREST
================================================================================

This chapter covers the interconnected world of fractions, decimals,
percentages, and financial mathematics (simple & compound interest).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 1: Share & Slice Café (Easy)
Route: /game/share-slice-cafe
Topic: Fractions Basics (Equal Sharing)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY A PIZZA CAFÉ?
  Fractions are fundamentally about SHARING. Cutting a pizza into equal
  slices is the most universally understood example. Students use a
  tap-to-serve interaction — tap a slice to pick it up, then tap a
  friend to deliver it — building fraction intuition through hands-on,
  tactile gameplay rather than abstract sliders.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  A 3D pizza sits on a café counter with friends standing around it. Each
  slice is individually selectable with a golden highlight on hover. The
  student taps a slice (it lifts and highlights in gold), then taps a
  friend to serve it to their plate. A bottom panel tracks per-person
  slice counts and remaining slices in real-time. The round succeeds
  only when ALL slices are distributed EQUALLY among all friends.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Fractions = sharing equally"
  2. Round 1 (Easy): 4 slices ÷ 2 friends → each gets 2 slices (½ each)
  3. Round 2: 6 slices ÷ 3 friends → each gets 2 slices (⅓ each)
  4. Round 3: 8 slices ÷ 4 friends → each gets 2 slices (¼ each)
  5. Round 4 (Tricky): 6 slices ÷ 2 friends → each gets 3 slices
  6. Tap a slice to select it (golden highlight), then tap a friend
  7. Use "Undo Last Serve" button to take back the last action
  8. Use "Start Over This Round" to reset the current round
  9. Incorrect distribution shows an encouragement message
  10. After all 4 rounds, complete the 8-question quiz

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • A fraction represents a part of a whole
  • Fair sharing means everyone gets the SAME number of slices
  • 2/6 = 1/3 (simplifying fractions through equal sharing)
  • Division as the basis of fractions: slices ÷ friends = slices per person
  • Tactile learning: physical serve actions reinforce the concept of
    distributing equally

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 2: Liquid Measure Lab (Medium)
Route: /game/liquid-measure-lab
Topic: Decimals & Conversions
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY LIQUID MEASUREMENT?
  Liquids naturally flow and divide — making them perfect for teaching
  decimals. Pouring 0.75 liters is more intuitive when you SEE it as
  three-quarters of a container. This game connects visual levels to
  decimal notation.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  3D beakers and containers with measurement markings. The student pours
  liquid between containers, reading decimal measurements. Challenges
  involve converting between fractions and decimals using visual levels.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Decimals are fractions in disguise!"
  2. Pour liquid to a target level (e.g., 0.75 liters)
  3. Convert: what fraction is 0.75? (answer: 3/4)
  4. Multiple rounds with different target values
  5. Complete the quiz

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • Decimals represent parts of a whole (0.5 = half)
  • Converting fractions to decimals: 3/4 = 0.75
  • Reading measurement scales
  • Place value in decimal notation

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GAME 3: Growing Money Garden (Advanced)
Route: /game/growing-money-garden
Topic: Simple & Compound Interest
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHY A MONEY GARDEN?
  Interest is "money that grows." A garden where trees grow based on
  interest rates makes the abstract concept of compounding visible.
  Students SEE the difference between linear growth (simple interest)
  and exponential growth (compound interest) as trees of different
  heights growing side by side.

WHAT HAPPENS:
  Three 3D "money trees" grow based on investment parameters:
  • Tree 1: Principal (doesn't grow)
  • Tree 2: Simple Interest (grows linearly)
  • Tree 3: Compound Interest (grows exponentially)
  
  The student sets principal, rate, and time. Trees grow proportionally.
  Calculation challenges ask students to compute SI and CI amounts.

HOW TO PLAY:
  1. Tutorial: "Watch your money grow!"
  2. Set investment parameters: Principal (₹1000), Rate (10%), Time (3 yrs)
  3. Click "Grow" — watch the three trees grow to different heights
  4. Read the SI and CI values displayed on each tree
  5. Answer calculation challenges: "What is the compound interest?"
  6. Compare SI vs CI for the same parameters
  7. Complete the quiz

WHAT IT TEACHES:
  • Simple Interest: SI = (P × R × T) / 100
  • Compound Interest: CI = P(1 + R/100)^T - P
  • Why compound interest grows faster over time
  • Real-world financial literacy

MATHEMATICAL CONNECTION:
  SI = P × R × T / 100
  CI = P × (1 + R/100)^T - P
  Amount = P + Interest

8. ASSESSMENT SYSTEM (AI-GENERATED QUIZZES)
================================================================================

After every game, students complete a mandatory 8-question quiz.

QUESTION GENERATION (Database-First System):
  • 540+ pre-stored questions across all 30 games (20+ per game)
  • Questions are loaded instantly from the database — no API calls needed
  • Shuffled randomly each session for variety
  • Difficulty-matched: easy, medium, and hard tiers per game
  • Written in plain, conversational English (no raw symbols)
  • Real-world scenarios (e.g., "A shop offers a 20% discount on a
    ₹500 item. How much do you save?")
  • LaTeX formulas used ONLY for complex mathematical expressions
  • Fallback chain: Database → AI generation → Curated static questions

QUESTION FORMAT:
  • Multiple choice (4 options)
  • Each question has an explanation and optional hint
  • Difficulty adapts to the student's grade and game difficulty level

SCORING:
  • Stars earned based on accuracy:
    - 3 stars: ≥ 80% correct
    - 2 stars: ≥ 60% correct
    - 1 star: ≥ 40% correct
    - 0 stars: < 40% correct
  • Completing with ≥ 50% accuracy unlocks the next difficulty level

WHY EMI?

WHAT IT TEACHES:
